The concept

Treat the ship like a patient.

Modern medicine keeps people alive through continuous observation, early diagnosis and timely treatment. Digital Healthcare Engineering applies the same discipline to steel — across the entire life of a structure, not just at scheduled check-ups.

MedicineEngineering

Vital signs

Heart rate, blood pressure, temperature — sampled continuously, not annually.

Sensor streams

Strain, vibration, corrosion, temperature and motion — measured on the structure, at sea, around the clock.

Examination

Symptoms are observed, tested and given a name.

Diagnosis

Anomalies are detected, located and identified: crack, dent, corrosion — the moment they begin.

Prognosis

How the condition will develop if nothing is done.

Remaining useful life

Physics and AI project damage growth and the time left before limit states are reached.

Treatment

The right procedure, at the right moment — not too late, not too often.

Intervention

Maintenance planned and executed before failure — in the right port, with the right parts.

The challenge

Steel doesn't complain. It fails.

Ships and offshore structures age in silence — corrosion thins plate, fatigue grows cracks, storms leave dents. Inspections are periodic; degradation is not. Failures still take operators by surprise, and surprise at sea is expensive.

Unmonitored structure No signal ▮
Progressive

Corrosion

Plate thickness quietly lost year after year, between dry-dockings — invisible until a gauge, or a leak, finds it.

Cumulative

Fatigue

Millions of wave cycles concentrate at welds and details. Cracks initiate long before anyone is scheduled to look.

Sudden

Extreme events

Storms, slamming, collisions and groundings leave damage that needs assessment now — not at the next survey.

Periodic inspection sees a structure a few days per decade. AETHERION watches it every second of its life.

Origin

Born in research. Built for the fleet.

Digital Healthcare Engineering didn't start as a product pitch. It began as a research framework — and grew into one.

2021

The framework is proposed

Digital Healthcare Engineering is articulated as a new discipline: lifetime healthcare for engineering structures, by direct analogy with modern medicine — monitor, diagnose, predict, intervene.

2022

The research deepens

Methods for in-service monitoring, damage diagnosis, prognosis and intervention planning are developed and published across multiple structure classes.

2023 — 2025

The community grows

Journal special issues, international collaboration and academia–industry projects carry DHE from concept toward practice, structure class by structure class.

Today

AETHERION

The framework becomes a product: one platform for the lifetime healthcare of ships and offshore structures — sensors, twin, diagnosis and decisions in a single loop.
